# Fixture and Test-Data Policy

- Prefer generated bytes and local HTTP scenarios over captured public media.
- Keep fixtures deterministic, minimal, and usable without network access.
- Do not store account cookies, tokens, signed URLs, personal data, or private
  responses in the repository.
- A captured response must record its source, capture date, applicable license,
  sanitization method, and the reference behavior it proves.
- Tests must fail with the scenario and request sequence needed to reproduce the
  failure.
- Large or ambiguous-license fixtures require review before they are added.
- Differential Python-oracle output may be generated during migration, but the
  Go product and Python-free gate must never invoke that oracle.
